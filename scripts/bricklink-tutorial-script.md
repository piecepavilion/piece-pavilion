## SECTION 4: UNDERSTANDING LISTINGS (5:00 - 6:15)

**[Screen: A store's listing page for an item]**

> "Alright, you've found what you want. Before you add it to your cart, let me walk you through what you're looking at.
>
> **Condition** — 'New' means the piece has never been assembled. It came from a sealed set and went straight into the seller's inventory. 'Used' means it was previously built with. And honestly? For most building purposes, used LEGO is perfectly fine. It's injection-molded plastic — it doesn't wear out from one assembly. Save your 'new' budget for display pieces or gifts.
>
> **Quantity** — How many the seller has. If you need 47 dark tan 1x2 plates for your desert MOC, make sure the store has 47.
>
> **Price** — Set by each seller individually. Prices vary, so it's worth checking a few stores. More on that in a second.
>
> **Seller Feedback** — This is your trust signal. A number and a percentage — like '2,847 orders, 100% praise.' Look for sellers above 98% with a meaningful number of ratings. A store with 5 orders and 100% praise tells you less than a store with 500 orders and 99.8%."

---

## SECTION 5: PLACING YOUR FIRST ORDER (6:15 - 8:00)

**[Screen: Walk through the full order flow]**

> "Here's where it all comes together. Let me walk you through an actual purchase, step by step.
>
> **Step 1: Add to cart.** Find your item, pick the store, click 'Add to Cart.' Simple.
>
> **[Demonstrate adding an item]**
>
> Now here's the thing that trips up most beginners: **each store has its own cart.** If you're buying from three different stores, you'll have three separate checkouts, three separate shipments, and three separate shipping charges. This is why consolidating your order into one store saves you real money.
>
> **Step 2: Check the store's minimum.** A lot of sellers have a minimum purchase amount — maybe $2, $5, sometimes more. You'll see this in their store terms. If you're under the minimum, add a few more pieces to your cart. You were going to need them eventually anyway.
>
> **[Show the cart / store terms]**
>
> **Step 3: Checkout.** When you're ready, submit your order. Now — and this is different from Amazon — **you don't pay immediately.** The seller receives your order, calculates shipping based on the weight and your location, and sends you an invoice.
>
> **Step 4: Pay the invoice.** You'll get a notification when the invoice is ready. Most stores take PayPal — some accept credit cards. Review the total, pay, and you're done.
>
> **Step 5: Wait for your bricks.** Domestic orders typically arrive in 3-7 days. You'll usually get a notification when it ships.
>
> And that's it. You just bought LEGO on BrickLink. Welcome to the club — your wallet may never recover."

## SECTION 6: PRO TIPS (8:00 - 9:30)

**[Screen: Various BrickLink pages as referenced]**

> "Now that you've got the basics, here are a few tricks that will save you time and money:
>
> **Tip 1: Use the Price Guide.** Every item on BrickLink has a price guide that shows what it's actually been selling for — not just what people are listing it at. This is your cheat sheet for knowing if a price is fair.
>
> **[Show the price guide for an item]**
>
> **Tip 2: Build a Wanted List.** This is one of BrickLink's best features. If you're working on a project and need a bunch of specific parts, create a Wanted List. Add everything you need, and BrickLink will help you find stores that carry multiple items from your list. One order instead of twelve. Your shipping costs will thank you.
>
> **[Show how to create/add to a Wanted List]**
>
> **Tip 3: Consolidate your orders.** I said it before, but it's worth repeating. Shipping from multiple stores adds up fast. Find a seller with a big inventory that carries most of what you need, and buy it all from one place. Stores like ours at Piece Pavilion carry thousands of items specifically so you can do this.
>
> **Tip 4: Read the store terms.** Every store has a terms page — shipping methods, processing time, return policy, minimum order. Take 30 seconds to read it before you buy. No surprises that way.
>
> **Tip 5: Don't be afraid to message the seller.** Have a question about condition? Wondering if they have an item that's not listed? Just send them a message. Good sellers respond quickly and are happy to help. If they don't respond — that tells you something too."
